Wie ist eine mündliche Englisch-Prüfung (P5) aufgebaut?	<b>Teil 1 (~10 Min):</b> Präsentation/Vortrag zu einem vorgegebenen Thema ODER Bildbeschreibung + Stellungnahme. Monologisch, zusammenhängend sprechen.<br><b>Teil 2 (~10-20 Min):</b> Prüfungsgespräch — Fragen zum Thema, Vertiefung, ggf. neues Material/Bild. Dialogisch, auf Fragen eingehen, Position vertreten.<br><b>Bewertung:</b> Inhalt (Argumentation, Wissen, Materialauswertung) + Sprache (Grammatik, Wortschatz, Aussprache, Flüssigkeit, Kommunikationsstrategien).	englisch::pruefungsformat
Useful Phrases: Bildbeschreibung	<b>Einleitung:</b> "The picture/photo shows…", "In the foreground/background we can see…", "The image depicts…"<br><b>Personen:</b> "The person seems to be…", "They appear to be [verb+ing]…", "Judging by their expression, they might feel…"<br><b>Ort/Atmosphäre:</b> "The setting is…", "It creates an atmosphere of…", "The overall mood is…"<br><b>Interpretation:</b> "This could represent…", "It might symbolize…", "This makes me think of…"<br><b>Verbindung zu Themen:</b> "This relates to the broader topic of…", "It raises questions about…"	englisch::useful_phrases
Useful Phrases: Meinung äußern und argumentieren	<b>Eigene Meinung:</b> "In my opinion…", "From my perspective…", "I strongly believe that…", "As far as I'm concerned…"<br><b>Argumente einleiten:</b> "First of all…", "Moreover/Furthermore…", "Another important aspect is…", "On top of that…"<br><b>Gegenposition:</b> "On the other hand…", "However, one could argue that…", "Admittedly…", "While I understand the argument that…, I still think…"<br><b>Schluss:</b> "All things considered…", "To sum up…", "Weighing the pros and cons…", "Ultimately, I believe…"	englisch::useful_phrases
Useful Phrases: Diskussion und Interaktion	<b>Nachfragen:</b> "Could you elaborate on that?", "What exactly do you mean by…?"<br><b>Zustimmen:</b> "I completely agree with…", "That's a valid point."<br><b>Widersprechen:</b> "I see your point, but…", "I'm afraid I disagree because…", "That may be true, however…"<br><b>Überleitung:</b> "That leads me to another point…", "Speaking of which…", "This connects to…"<br><b>Umschreibung (wenn Wort fehlt):</b> "What I mean is…", "In other words…", "It's a kind of…", "Let me put it differently…"	englisch::useful_phrases
Topic: Globalization — Key Arguments	<b>Pro:</b> Economic growth, cultural exchange, access to global markets, technological innovation spreads faster, poverty reduction in developing countries.<br><b>Contra:</b> Growing inequality (rich-poor gap), loss of cultural identity, exploitation of cheap labor, environmental damage, dependency on global supply chains (fragile, as COVID showed).<br><b>Key vocabulary:</b> interdependence, free trade, outsourcing, cultural homogenization, digital divide, supply chain, multinational corporations, sweatshops, fair trade.	englisch::topics::globalization
Topic: Technology & Social Media — Key Arguments	<b>Pro:</b> Connectivity, access to information, democratic participation (citizen journalism), education opportunities, economic innovation.<br><b>Contra:</b> Privacy concerns (data collection, surveillance), echo chambers and filter bubbles, cyberbullying, addiction/screen time, spread of misinformation, mental health impact (social comparison, FOMO).<br><b>Key vocabulary:</b> algorithm, digital literacy, data privacy, artificial intelligence, screen time, misinformation, echo chamber, whistleblower, surveillance capitalism.	englisch::topics::technology
Topic: Individual and Society — Key Arguments	<b>Identity:</b> Shaped by culture, family, media, peer groups. Tension between conformity and individuality. Coming-of-age narratives.<br><b>Discrimination:</b> Racism, sexism, homophobia — structural vs. individual. Intersectionality. Positive change through movements (BLM, #MeToo).<br><b>Freedom:</b> Individual freedom vs. social responsibility. Limits of freedom (hate speech, public health). Role of government.<br><b>Key vocabulary:</b> identity, conformity, peer pressure, prejudice, discrimination, diversity, inclusion, empowerment, civil rights, systemic racism.	englisch::topics::society
Topic: Environment & Sustainability — Key Arguments	<b>Problems:</b> Climate change (greenhouse gases, rising temperatures), biodiversity loss, deforestation, ocean pollution, resource depletion.<br><b>Solutions:</b> Renewable energy, circular economy, carbon taxes, international agreements (Paris Agreement), individual action (reduce, reuse, recycle), sustainable agriculture.<br><b>Debates:</b> Economic growth vs. sustainability, role of developing countries, greenwashing, climate justice, intergenerational responsibility.<br><b>Key vocabulary:</b> carbon footprint, fossil fuels, renewable energy, biodiversity, ecosystem, sustainability, greenhouse effect, carbon neutral, emissions.	englisch::topics::environment
Topic: The UK and USA in a Changing World	<b>UK:</b> Brexit (sovereignty vs. economic impact, immigration debate, Northern Ireland), Commonwealth legacy, multicultural society, class system, monarchy debate.<br><b>USA:</b> American Dream (myth vs. reality, social mobility, inequality), political polarization (Democrats/Republicans), gun control debate, immigration, racial tensions, global superpower role.<br><b>Shared themes:</b> Post-colonial responsibility, special relationship UK-USA, role of English as global language, media influence on politics.<br><b>Key vocabulary:</b> sovereignty, immigration policy, cultural diversity, social mobility, polarization, civil liberties, constitutional rights.	englisch::topics::uk_usa
